About

I am a hospitality researcher whose work examines how consumers interpret, evaluate, and remember lodging experiences across physical, digital, and technology-mediated settings.

My research focuses on consumer behavior in hospitality and tourism, with particular attention to lodging experience, experience design, boutique hotels, and AI-related behavioral questions. Across these areas, I study how service environments, interface design, and emerging technologies shape perception, decision making, and experience outcomes.

Methodologically, I combine experimental design, computational text analysis, machine learning, and advanced statistical modeling to investigate both theoretical and applied questions in hospitality management.

Research Areas

My work connects hospitality experience, lodging strategy, consumer behavior, big data analytics, and AI applications.

Consumer Behavior in Hospitality

Examines how consumers respond to service design, digital interfaces, and hospitality environments across the decision journey.

Lodging Experience and Boutique Hotels

Studies boutique hotel experiences, physical environments, event design, and guest evaluations in lodging contexts.

Experience Design in Hospitality

Explores how symbolic, sensory, and operational design features influence emotion, memory, and experiential meaning.

Big Data Analytics

Uses text mining, topic modeling, and large-scale review analytics to generate insights for hospitality and tourism research.

AI-Powered Behavioral Modeling

Investigates trust, transparency, and behavioral responses to AI-enabled systems and content in service settings.

Applied Hospitality Insights

Bridges academic research and industry relevance through data-driven reports and managerial interpretation.
